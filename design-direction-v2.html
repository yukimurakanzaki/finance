What changed, and which pain it answers

1

Standing strip replaces the three chips. Safe-to-spend is the hero; spent-today, wallet balance, and week-remaining sit under it. Answers “can I buy lunch, from which account” on one screen (F1, T4). The misleading day-“Balance” chip is gone.

2

“Today” anchor pill. Off-today the pill becomes a tappable “Back to today”, so you always know which day you're on (Today-page pains #8).

3

Sentence-case section headers. 13px semibold ink-2 text. The 10px-uppercase treatment is retired from headers and kept only for data labels inside the strip (D3).

4

Rows, not boxes. Flush 56px rows with one hairline separator; pressed state uses bg-2. The only card on the screen is the standing strip (D2, D6, D7).

5

Numbers gutter. tabular-nums, right-aligned fixed column, sign carries direction — no red/green noise on every row (D5). Abbreviations use jt/M-miliar per Indonesian convention (T3).

6

One-action FAB. Expense is the 90% case and becomes one tap; income/transfer live inside the sheet as a segment. The stray blue/red accent circles are gone (D8).

7

Real icons. Inline SVG, 1.7px stroke, 20px grid — no emoji/glyph rendering lottery (D4). Labels 11px minimum; all targets ≥44px (D6).
